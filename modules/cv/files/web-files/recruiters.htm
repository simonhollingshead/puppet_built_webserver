CV · Information for Recruiters

Firstly, I want to say that I am recruiter-friendly. I recognise the potential benefits of talking to a recruiter, and there are a couple of recruiters that I've met in my working career that have helped me progress faster than I otherwise would. I believe that recruiters can have value to me, and therefore I'm generally willing to hear them out on what they have to say.

That being said, however, I have personal values and morals that I prefer to stand by, and I believe that ethics are very important. I am also generally busy either with work or with personal projects, and prefer approaches to be mindful of this. For these reasons, I have a list of requests which, if followed, will be more likely to lead to a positive response.


Wait long enough.
A job measured in days or weeks is definitely not a job I'm looking to leave.

Email first.
Drop me an email introducing the opportunity. Due to busyness, I might not get back to you within working hours, or even on the same day.

No work calls.
If I'm at work, I'm being paid to give 100% of my attention to my employer. I consider it awkward and imposing to receive recruitment calls at work.

Be clear.
Finding a message that just asks me to call with no suggestion of what it's for is a waste. Please give me enough information to have an idea of whether it's whether it's worth me making space in my day to make a call.

Understand me.
I won't just settle for any tech job. I really want to do something I'll enjoy. I got my degree in specific subjects, and my CV and LinkedIn detail skills I've picked up while working. Please also ask questions to expand that knowledge further.

Have ethics.
I really dislike the members of the profession that give it a bad name. I take the time to escalate these things or, if dissatisfied, will just blanket block a recruitment firm. Don't lie to me, misrepresent things, or send my CV to companies without my permission. I'm reasonable as long as you are.
